My "day job" profession as an electrical engineer keeps me focused on hardware systems, but I've had a long-term interest in human-computer interaction, in which web/mobile interfaces provide a huge amount of ___. I've self-taught myself simple HTML/CSS for creating personal websites with static content, but the design has mostly been minimalistic. While I believe simplicity is beautiful, I realize design is intricate in providing a good user experience.

Admittedly, I am not a great speaker or one who can quickly translate thoughts into clear, concise verbioge. Writing allows me to visualize complete sentences and construct them in ways to convey my perspective. I'm a slow writer, but that extra time allows a more graceful poet.

Anyway, I wanted to create a unique domain name for my personal website. My given name, Hieu Nguyen, is far too common and leaves no room for differentiation. So I settled on a combination of words, which could abbreviate to my initials (HMN). And with this obscure naming methodology, NiftyHedgehog was born.

I wish I could say that I'm a hedgehog enthusiast, but the truth is I've had very little interaction with hedgehogs in the real world. The website is setup to incur minimal cost. Hosted entirely on GitHub, email service provided through Zoho Mail (and redirected to Gmail), built with a responsive Bootstrap framework, projects through GitHub pages, blog through Wordpress, community through Disqus, Google Analytics, monetization with ChangeTip digital currency.

Jekyll’s strength is its simplicity and minimalism, giving you just what you need to create a content-focused website that doesn’t need much dynamic user interaction — and no more. This makes it perfect for your blog and portfolio and also worth considering for a simple client website.
